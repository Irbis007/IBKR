Market Pulse

We’re seeing an all-out rally this morning. All major indexes hit new highs, riding off Trump’s re-election and what people are already calling the "Trump Trade." Dow’s up 3.57%, S&P 500 gained 2.53%, and Nasdaq’s also charging ahead at 2.95%. Treasury yields are spiking, and the dollar’s looking strong. This market confidence is telling us investors are ready to back U.S. assets, expecting economic growth with a possible deregulation push.

Financial Stocks to Watch

Brokerage firms are having a standout day. Charles Schwab, Interactive Brokers, and others in the financial space are up significantly. It seems like investors are betting on a friendlier regulatory landscape, so it could be worth taking a closer look at any positions you have in financials, especially those benefiting from trading volume or a return of retail traders.

Tech is Running Hot

Tech is up across the board. Tesla, Nvidia, and Google are leading the charge. Tesla’s been particularly strong, and Nvidia isn’t far behind. This sector is benefiting from the overall rally, but it also seems to be getting a bump from anticipation around tech-friendly policies. If you’re looking to ride some momentum, these are names to keep on your radar today.

EV Sector Looking Promising

We’re seeing impressive movement in the EV stocks — Ford, GM, Tesla, Rivian — all performing well. The market seems to be speculating that the new administration might make changes around tariffs and production regulations, which could directly impact supply chain costs. It’s worth keeping an eye on this sector if you want to make a play around these policy changes.

Beauty Stocks on the Rise

On the consumer side, e.l.f. Beauty had a big earnings beat and raised its annual outlook. The company mentioned it might raise prices due to tariffs, but demand seems to be holding strong. There’s been a noticeable jump in its stock price, so if you’re interested in consumer goods as a non-traditional growth angle, this could be worth a look.

Big Picture Strategy

Short-term, there’s a lot of energy in the market. Some analysts are suggesting you reduce hedges and deploy more cash to take advantage of this rally. There’s a lot of optimism right now, so it could be a good moment to get more aggressive, especially in the tech and financial spaces. Longer-term, EVs and consumer goods (like beauty and cosmetics) might be where you want to hold steady. Policies around tariffs and environmental regulations are going to affect these sectors, so they’re worth tracking closely as we get more clarity on the administration’s direction.
